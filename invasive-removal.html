The Impact of a Day

Once a region of non-native invasive plants is mechanically removed, previously opressed native plants will be exposed.

The non-native invasive plants growing in an area vary greatly and an extensive profile of those growing in Pennsylvania can be found in the DEP Invasive Plant Field Guide.

Reclaiming the riparian zone

Multiflora rose bushes had been enjoying a nice stay next to the pond. Plenty of sun and water available at this site. Dead ash trees scatter the area, no longer providing any shade.




Easy targeting in the winter

Multiflora rose bushes growing uninhibited. Perennial vegetation has disappeared for the winter, creating greater access.




A powerful duo dominating

A tangled combination of privet and multiflora rose. The privet is located in the center of the area removed, with the rose climbing on it. Serving as a trellis, the privet helps the multiflora rose make a home in the trees.




A sunny Ash tree graveyard

With no canopy providing any shade, the privet and multiflora rose achieved a truly dominant form. Once removed, seed from native flowering plants was spread throughout. A variety of native fruiting trees were planted to begin ushering the next succession of forest.
