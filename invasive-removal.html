The Impact of a Day

Once a region of non-native invasive plants is removed, native plants previously oppressed will be exposed. The non-native invasive plants growing in an area vary greatly and an extensive profile of those growing in Pennsylvania can be found in the DEP Invasive Plant Field Guide.

Use the slider bar to experience the transition.

Reclaiming the riparian zone

Multi flora rose bushes had been enjoying a nice stay next to the pond. Plenty of sun and water available at this site. Dead Ash trees scatter the area, no longer providing any shade.




Easy targeting in the winter

Multi flora rose bushes growing uninhibited. Perennial vegetation has disappeared for the winter, creating greater access.




A powerful duo dominating

A tangled combination of privet and multi flora rose. The privet is located in the center of the area removed, with the rose climbing on it. Serving as a trellis, the privet helps the multi flora rose make a home in the trees.




A sunny Ash tree graveyard

With no canopy providing any shade, the privet and multi flora rose achieved a truly dominant form. Once removed, seed from native flowering plants was spread throughout. A variety of native fruiting trees were planted to begin ushering the next succession of forest.
