Natures output

Forests are already incredibly productive. With the stability that native plants establish, a richer ecosystem will take hold.


Wild Game

Ruffed grouse, wild turkey, and white-tailed deer will find an abundance of food in a thriving ecosystem.


Firewood

An economical means to heat your home.


Mushrooms

Fungi play an important role cycling nutrients. A truly gourmet food, these sought after treats are highly nutritious and a key indicator of a healthy forest. A forest is a great place to cultivate mushrooms, or one can forage for numerous wild crafted mushrooms including morel, chanterelle, chicken of the woods (pictured here), puff balls, and oyster.


Medicinals

Ginseng (pictured here), golden seal, black cohosh, blue cohosh, blood root, and false unicorn are just a few of the numerous plants that are traditionally used for healing.


Tree tapping

Did you know you can tap black walnut and birch trees? A forest free of invasive plants enables one to approach trees to harvest their nutritious sap.
